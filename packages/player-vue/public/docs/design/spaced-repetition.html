ğŸ”¬ The Science

"To remember something forever, review it at increasing intervals just before you would forget it."

Spaced repetition exploits a fundamental property of human memory: retrieval strengthens storage. Each time you successfully recall something, the memory becomes more durable and the interval before you need to recall it again gets longer.

The Spacing Effect

Discovered by Hermann Ebbinghaus in 1885. Information reviewed at spaced intervals is remembered far better than information crammed in a single session. This is one of the most robust findings in cognitive psychology.

  • Extends the interval until next review
  • Moves information toward permanent storage

Why Fibonacci?

  • Natural growth: Each number is ~1.618x the previous (golden ratio)
  • Biological resonance: Memory decay roughly follows similar curves
  • Practical spacing: Early reviews close together, later reviews spread out
  • Simple computation: No complex calculations needed

Why LEGOs, Not Phrases?

  • LEGOs are the learning unit: The thing the brain must acquire
  • Phrases are contexts: Different ways to practice the same LEGO
  • Flexibility: Same LEGO can appear in varied phrase contexts
  • Efficiency: One LEGO mastery = all its phrases covered

Key Insight

When a LEGO is due for review, we select a phrase (from the eternal pool) as the practice context. The phrase provides variety; the LEGO is what we're tracking.

Why Interleave?

  • Context switching: Prevents rote memorization
  • Desirable difficulty: Mixed practice is harder but more effective
  • Natural spacing: New learning interspersed with review
  • Time efficiency: Review happens "for free" within new learning

Blocked vs. Interleaved Practice

Blocked: AAAA BBBB CCCC (feels easier, worse retention)

Interleaved: ABCA BCAB CABC (feels harder, better retention)

Retirement Criteria

  • Skip number threshold: e.g., skip > 89
  • Time-based: Interval exceeds ~60 days
  • Performance-based: Consistent low-latency responses

After Retirement: The Seed Bank

Retired LEGOs enter the Seed Bank and become available for:

  • Listening exercises: Full seed playback
  • Eternal phrase comprehension: Target-only audio
  • Accelerated listening: Bulk input mode

The Goal

Move LEGOs from "needs active practice" to "solidly in long-term memory" as efficiently as possible. Spaced repetition is the engine; retirement is the destination.
